Chest X-Ray Pathology Detection

What is ThoraxAI?

ThoraxAI is an advanced artificial intelligence system developed by LungGuard AI that specializes in analyzing thoracic (chest) radiographs. Our deep learning model has been trained on thousands of annotated chest X-rays to detect various pulmonary and thoracic abnormalities with high accuracy. ThoraxAI assists radiologists by providing rapid preliminary assessments, helping to identify potential pathologies that might require urgent attention.

Chest X-Ray Abnormalities

1. Lung Diseases

Pneumonia

Infection causing lung inflammation and consolidation, appearing as areas of increased opacity in the lungs. Our AI detects patterns like lobar consolidation, bronchopneumonia, and interstitial patterns.

Tuberculosis (TB)

Bacterial infection causing cavities or nodules in the lungs, typically in the upper lobes. The model identifies apical opacities, cavitary lesions, and miliary patterns characteristic of TB.

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

Includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis, leading to lung hyperinflation and flattened diaphragms. The AI measures lung volumes and detects bullae formation.

Lung Cancer

Abnormal growth or mass in lung tissue, often appearing as solitary pulmonary nodules. The system evaluates nodule characteristics like spiculation and growth rate.

Pulmonary Edema

Fluid accumulation in the lungs, often due to heart failure, showing bilateral perihilar opacities. The AI detects Kerley B lines and bat-wing patterns.

Pneumothorax

Collapsed lung due to air leakage in the pleural space, visible as a visceral pleural line. The model is particularly sensitive to small apical pneumothoraces.

Pleural Effusion

Excess fluid in the pleural cavity surrounding the lungs, causing blunting of costophrenic angles. The system quantifies effusion size and evaluates for loculations.

Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD)

Scarring or fibrosis affecting lung tissue, appearing as reticular or nodular patterns. The AI classifies patterns like honeycombing and ground-glass opacities.

2. Heart-Related Conditions

Cardiomegaly

Enlarged heart, indicating conditions like heart failure, with a cardiothoracic ratio >50%. The AI automatically calculates cardiac silhouette measurements.

Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)

Fluid buildup in the lungs due to poor heart function, often with cephalization of pulmonary vessels. The system evaluates vascular redistribution and edema patterns.

Aortic Aneurysm

Abnormal widening of the aorta visible on X-ray as mediastinal widening or abnormal contours. The AI measures aortic diameter and tracks changes over time.

3. Bone and Rib Abnormalities

Rib Fractures

Broken ribs due to trauma, visible as discontinuities in the bony cortex. The model highlights fracture sites and counts affected ribs.

Scoliosis

Abnormal curvature of the spine affecting the chest cavity anatomy. The AI calculates Cobb angles for severity assessment.

Osteoporosis

Weak bones, visible through rib thinning and decreased bone density. The system evaluates cortical thinning and trabecular patterns.

4. Infections and Inflammatory Conditions

Bronchitis

Inflammation of the bronchial tubes, sometimes visible as thickened airway walls. The AI detects peribronchial cuffing and hyperinflation.

Sarcoidosis

Inflammatory disease causing enlarged lymph nodes and lung nodules in characteristic patterns. The model identifies hilar lymphadenopathy and perilymphatic nodules.

5. Foreign Objects and Medical Devices

Aspiration (Foreign Body)

Inhalation of objects into the lungs, common in children, often causing atelectasis. The system detects radiopaque foreign bodies and secondary changes.

Pacemaker or Medical Implants

Devices inserted for heart conditions, visible as metallic objects with leads in the chest. The AI verifies proper positioning and identifies lead fractures.
